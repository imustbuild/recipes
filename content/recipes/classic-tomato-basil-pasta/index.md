---
title: "Classic Tomato Basil Pasta"
description: "A simple, comforting pasta with fresh tomatoes, garlic, and basil. Ready in under 30 minutes."
servings: 4
tags: ["dinner", "italian", "vegetarian", "quick", "weeknight"]

times:
  prep_minutes: 10
  cook_minutes: 20

ingredients:
  - item: "spaghetti"
    amount: "1 lb"
  - item: "cherry tomatoes"
    amount: "2 pints"
    note: "halved"
  - item: "garlic"
    amount: "4 cloves"
    note: "minced"
  - item: "olive oil"
    amount: "1/4 cup"
  - item: "fresh basil"
    amount: "1 cup"
    note: "loosely packed"
  - item: "parmesan cheese"
    amount: "1/2 cup"
    note: "freshly grated"
  - item: "red pepper flakes"
    amount: "1/4 tsp"
  - item: "salt"
    amount: "to taste"
  - item: "black pepper"
    amount: "to taste"

instructions:
  - "Bring a large pot of salted water to boil. Cook spaghetti according to package directions until al dente. Reserve 1 cup pasta water before draining."
  - "While pasta cooks, heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add garlic and red pepper flakes, cook for 30 seconds until fragrant."
  - "Add halved cherry tomatoes to the skillet. Cook for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until tomatoes soften and release their juices."
  - "Season with salt and pepper. Use a wooden spoon to gently crush some of the tomatoes."
  - "Add drained pasta to the skillet. Toss to combine, adding pasta water as needed to create a light sauce."
  - "Remove from heat. Add torn basil leaves and half the parmesan, toss to combine."
  - "Serve immediately with remaining parmesan on top."

notes: "For extra richness, add a tablespoon of butter when tossing the pasta. Works well with any pasta shape."

---

This is my go-to weeknight pasta. The key is using really good cherry tomatoes - they're sweeter and more flavorful than regular tomatoes, especially in winter.

Don't skip the pasta water! The starch helps the sauce cling to the noodles and creates that silky texture.
