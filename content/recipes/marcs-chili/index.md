servings: 8
tags: ["dinner", "slow-cooker", "make-ahead", "american"]

times:
  prep_minutes: 30
  cook_minutes: 480

ingredients:
  - item: "ground beef"
    amount: "1 lb"
  - item: "ground Italian sausage"
    amount: "1 lb"
  - item: "green bell pepper"
    amount: "1 large"
    note: "chunky chopped"
  - item: "sweet onion"
    amount: "1 large"
    note: "chunky chopped"
  - item: "serrano peppers"
    amount: "2 large"
    note: "seeds removed, finely chopped (or jalapeño)"
  - item: "garlic"
    amount: "5 cloves"
    note: "finely chopped"
  - item: "canned diced tomatoes"
    amount: "28 oz"
  - item: "canned kidney beans"
    amount: "28 oz"
  - item: "Worcestershire sauce"
    amount: "1/4 cup"
  - item: "fresh basil"
    amount: "1/4 cup"
    note: "finely chopped"
  - item: "fresh oregano"
    amount: "1/4 cup"
    note: "finely chopped"
  - item: "paprika"
    amount: "1 tbsp"
  - item: "cumin"
    amount: "1 tbsp"
  - item: "cayenne pepper"
    amount: "1 tsp"
  - item: "chili powder"
    amount: "1 tsp"
  - item: "salt & pepper"
    note: "for seasoning meat"

instructions:
  - "In a large skillet, brown sausage and remove to crock pot. Brown ground beef, adding salt and pepper to taste. Remove to crock pot."
  - "In meat drippings, sauté the onions, garlic, serrano peppers and bell peppers. Remove to crock pot."
  - "Add remaining ingredients to crock pot and stir. Cook on low for many hours, overnight is great."
  - "Set to warm and serve all day over Fritos and topped with green onions, sour cream, cheddar cheese and jalapeños."

notes: "For serving: Fritos, sour cream, shredded cheddar cheese, chopped green onions, and jarred jalapeños. Overnight cooking gives the best flavor."

This is my signature chili recipe - it's been a crowd favorite for over a decade. The combination of beef and Italian sausage gives it incredible depth, and the fresh herbs make all the difference.

The secret is letting it cook low and slow overnight. The flavors meld together beautifully.
